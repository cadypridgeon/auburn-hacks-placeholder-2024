Auburn Hacks

March 29-30, 2025

About

Auburn Hacks is a 24-hour event where students from Auburn University and universities in the Southeast come together to build technical projects. We provide workshops to give students the tools they need to develop outstanding projects, and at the end of the event, participants submit their projects for judging to win prizes. We promote an encouraging atmosphere through events and activities that foster a friendly community for all participants and create opportunities for them to network with the companies that sponsor us to make the event possible. Please join us on March 29-30, 2025, for the largest hackathon in Alabama!

FAQ

What is a hackathon?

A hackathon is an event that typically lasts one or two days, where people come together to collaborate and create a project of their choosing. Hackathons are usually coding events, but programming experience is not required!

What goes on at Auburn Hacks?

At Auburn Hacks, hundreds of people come together to learn about coding and build technical projects. This year, we invite those interested in creating a project with hardware to participate. Additionally, if you have no computer science or engineering experience, please join us! We have many resources for you to learn more about the tech field. Outside of working on their projects, participants can attend workshops, activities, and tech talks to learn about computer science and build a community.

When and where is the hackathon?

More details will be released as we get closer to the event. Stay tuned and join the Discord for updates!

Do I need to know how to code to participate?

No, not at all! We want the hackathon to be a learning experience for everyone, so if you are interested in computer science, please join us! We ensure there is something for everyone to learn.

Are there any rules?

Our biggest rule is that all work must be done at Auburn Hacks. Also, everyone who participates must follow the MLH Code of Conduct.

Are there any COVID-19 regulations?

Auburn Hacks will be held in accordance with university policies. Currently, the hackathon will be in-person, and masks will not be required. However, if you are exposed to COVID-19, please follow the CDC guidelines to determine whether you should participate.

Have more questions?

If you have any other questions, please email us!
